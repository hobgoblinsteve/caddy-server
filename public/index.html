How Caddie adjusts: headwind adds ~1% per mph to the number you need to cover; helping wind takes off ~0.5% per mph. Cold air below 70°F adds ~2 yds per 10°F. Rain adds ~5 yds. Rough and deep rough add club because clean contact is harder.

How Caddie learns: after each shot, tell it Short, Long, Flush, or Mishit. Short/Long nudges that club's number by 2 yds (capped at ±12, so one bad day can't wreck your bag). Flush slowly confirms your number back toward baseline. Mishits are ignored — a chunked wedge says nothing about your wedge distance. And if you're net two or more shots short (or long) today, Caddie leans that way for the rest of the round, then resets tomorrow.
